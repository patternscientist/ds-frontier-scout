# Skeptical Audit

Batch 002 adversarial audit, 2026-05-15:

- Open status: uncertain and too broad. Classic KDS sources support the idea that higher-dimensional kinetic extent maintenance is difficult, but Batch 002 did not verify a line-level open problem for the exact target.
- Why might this not actually be open? Specific 3D measures such as diameter, width, hull, or enclosing boxes may have different known bounds; one of them may already have the desired KDS in a later paper.
- Why might it be too saturated? It is not saturated in a popularity sense. The risk is that old broad kinetic-geometry questions are too technical and fragmented to become a crisp AI-assisted target.
- Smallest meaningful subproblem: 3D kinetic diameter or 3D kinetic width under algebraic motion of bounded degree, with an explicit event-count/locality/responsiveness benchmark.
- Best use after audit: background context until narrowed to one measure and one motion model.
- Weak-source flag: handbook-level and metadata-only sources are insufficient. Need exact statements from Basch-Guibas-Hershberger and Agarwal-Guibas-Hershberger-Veach, plus later kinetic proximity/extent work.
- Why might automation fail or mislead? Finite event simulations can find bad examples but cannot certify asymptotic KDS event bounds; generated instances may overfit low-degree trajectories.
- What would falsify interest? A modern survey or primary paper giving tight higher-dimensional bounds for the chosen measure, or evidence that the only open cases require very specialized algebraic-geometry machinery.
- Primary sources to check next: Basch-Guibas-Hershberger 1999; Agarwal-Guibas-Hershberger-Veach 2001; post-2016 kinetic diameter/width/proximity papers.
