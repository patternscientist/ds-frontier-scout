# Skeptical Audit

Why this might not actually be open:

- The 2012 source explicitly says the optimal randomized competitive ratio lies between `1.5` and `1.6`, but a post-2012 improvement could be hidden under online algorithms or advice/variant terminology.
- Projective/list-factoring lower bounds do not rule out all online algorithms.

Why this might be too saturated:

- It is classic and old rather than currently crowded. The narrow gap may be old because it is intrinsically hard.

Why automatic evaluation might fail:

- Finite list sizes and finite-state randomized algorithms may not lift to arbitrary list length.
- LP/game certificates can become enormous and model-specific.

What would falsify interest:

- A newer primary source improves the `1.5`--`1.6` gap or proves COMB optimal beyond projective algorithms.
- Search only produces algorithms equivalent to known projective mixtures.

Primary sources to check before promotion:

- Ambuehl-Gaertner-von Stengel 2012.
- Post-2012 list update, randomized online algorithms, and advice-complexity variants.
