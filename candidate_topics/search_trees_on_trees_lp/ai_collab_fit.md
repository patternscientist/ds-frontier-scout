# AI Collaboration Fit

Theorem-project suitability: 5/5 for a narrow LP/subclass theorem; 3/5 for the full polynomial-time optimality problem.

Good staged workflow:

1. Blind prompt: define STTs recursively and ask for a polynomial-time exact algorithm or proof of integrality for a chosen topology class.
2. Frontier document: introduce Golinsky's LP, root rounding, the 2025 counterexamples, stars, paths, and almost-stars.
3. Literature mode: use the 2020-2025 STT papers, Berendsohn thesis context, and polyhedral/combinatorial-optimization tools.

AI is likely useful for:

- simplifying the LP constraints into equivalent forms;
- proposing missing valid inequalities;
- turning small fractional vertices into human-readable obstructions;
- searching for inductive proofs on stars, paths, and edge-diameter-3 trees;
- comparing DP formulations against LP relaxations.

This is a better theorem-project candidate than the Splay preorder topic because the target is newer, smaller, and has explicit counterexample artifacts.
