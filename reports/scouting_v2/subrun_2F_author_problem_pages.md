Status: not started.

Role: search author-maintained open-problem pages, course pages, invited-talk pages, and personal notes for precise data-structure or nearby algorithmic-theory problems that may not appear in polished survey lists.

## Assigned Source Pool

Allowed sources:

- Author-maintained open-problem pages.
- Research-group pages with named open problems and stable source metadata.
- Course notes, lecture notes, and invited-talk notes by domain experts when they explicitly state open problems.
- Personal publication pages when they include problem statements, errata, follow-up notes, or "future work" pages.

Out of scope:

- Aggregated web lists without clear authorship or source trail.
- Secondary blog posts unless they point to a primary author-maintained statement.
- Broad web search results that do not land on an author-maintained or expert-maintained source.

## Required Checks Before Promotion

- Check `candidate_topics/` slugs and `reports/top_20_shortlist.md`.
- Record the author/page owner, page title, URL, date searched, and any linked primary paper.
- Verify whether the page is current enough to trust; stale pages need `open_status: uncertain` unless freshness checks support the gap.
- Run a last-24-month freshness check for each proposed candidate.
- Preserve exact wording only in short excerpts; otherwise paraphrase and distinguish source statements from scouting hypotheses.
- Do not promote more than five candidates; fewer is acceptable.
